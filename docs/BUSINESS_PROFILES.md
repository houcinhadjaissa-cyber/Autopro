# Autopro Business Profiles

This file defines how Autopro handles public business profiles, private business dashboards, account modules, verification, trust, branches, social content, supplier profiles, service provider profiles, hybrid profiles, shipping profiles, fleet profiles, dealer profiles, collector/museum profiles, influencer/media profiles, analytics, paid profile features, search ranking, privacy, and legal rules.

Autopro must be designed with a complete business profile backbone from day one, even if the first public launch starts simple.

No coding has started yet.

---

# 1. Business Profile Purpose

Autopro business profiles must help businesses present, promote, manage, and grow their work inside the platform.

Autopro is not only a simple product listing website.

Business profiles must support:

- Storefront presentation
- Public trust
- Product sales
- Service bookings
- Social posts
- Reviews
- Verification badges
- Branches
- Staff-controlled management
- Private dashboards
- Paid growth tools
- Analytics
- Client communication
- Repeat customers
- Fleet and professional accounts
- Future reusable platform structure

A business profile must work like a professional storefront and business control center.

Public users see the public profile.

The business owner and approved employees use the private dashboard.

---

# 2. Public Profile And Private Dashboard

Every business account should have a public profile where useful.

The public profile is the client-facing storefront.

The private dashboard is the owner and employee control area.

The public profile and private dashboard must be separate.

Clients can see the public profile.

The business owner can see and control both the public profile and private dashboard.

Approved employees can access only the dashboard sections allowed by their permissions.

Every business account must have a private dashboard.

The private dashboard must be automatically created based on the selected account type, modules, plan, country, permissions, and business needs.

---

# 3. Shipping Company Exception

Shipping companies are mainly operational tools inside Autopro.

Shipping companies must have direct business accounts with full dashboards and functionality.

Shipping companies do not need a full public marketplace profile by default.

For clients, shipping companies should appear mainly as delivery options during product orders.

A shipping option should show quick details such as:

- Shipping company name
- Delivery time estimate
- Price
- Route
- Pickup or delivery method
- Cash-on-delivery support if available
- Insurance support if available
- Claims/damage policy link
- Contact/support option where allowed

When a client selects a shipping company, the shipping details can expand into a note or extra detail area for that order.

After selection, the shipping company becomes linked to the client order, client dashboard, tracking flow, notifications, and messaging/support flow.

The client should not need to ask where the package is if tracking is available.

Autopro must automatically update tracking step by step through:

- Client dashboard
- Notifications
- Order timeline
- Messaging/support flow where needed

A business should first register as a general business account.

After registration, the business can activate one or more modules.

Modules may include:

- Supplier/seller module
- Service provider/garage module

- Shipping/logistics module
- Partner desk module

- Pickup desk module
- Fleet module
- Dealer module
- Collector/museum module

A business can activate multiple modules.

One business profile can contain multiple modules.

Example:

```txt
One garage can sell products, offer services, run fleet clients, publish posts, manage branches, and run paid promotions from one business profile.
```

- Business type
- Contact method

- Country
- Basic identity information
- Required legal or verification information where applicable
- Return/service/warranty terms where legally required

Mobile businesses do not need a fixed shop location if they operate by service area.

Private warehouses and high-value collectors can hide exact addresses where safety requires.

Profile completion percentage must be shown to business owners.

Example:

```txt

```

Profile completion should affect:

- Search ranking
- Trust
- Visibility
- Recommendations
- Growth suggestions

Incomplete profiles can operate normally and can pay for plans.

However, incomplete profiles should not receive the best reach until they improve profile quality.

Autopro must guide businesses step by step to complete their profiles.

The system should explain how each completed section helps visibility, trust, ranking, client confidence, or conversion.

# 17. Shipping Company Approval Flow With Suppliers

Shipping companies must be able to control who uses their services.

A supplier can choose shipping companies from available options.

If a shipping company accepts all eligible suppliers automatically, the supplier selection can become approved immediately.

The supplier should see a green approved status.

If the shipping company manually approves who it works with, the supplier request should show pending.

The supplier should see an orange pending status.

The system must automatically send a request to the shipping company.

The request should include the supplier profile link and relevant business information.

The shipping company can review the supplier, message them, contact them where allowed, accept, decline, or block.

If accepted, the supplier sees approved status and can show that shipping option under products.

If declined, the status stays declined or unavailable.

If blocked, the shipping company is removed from the supplier shipping choices and the supplier cannot request or contact that shipping company again through that flow unless allowed by admin or rules.

# 23. Booking And Availability Rules

Garages should not be forced to show a full booking calendar publicly.

Public availability can be hidden.

VIP/trusted clients can see more appointment options if the service provider allows it.

Service providers can hide exact availability and accept booking requests only.

When a provider accepts a booking, the provider must provide a date and time or available options.

The client can accept, decline, or request rescheduling.

Autopro must support fixed-price services.

Fixed-price service listings must explain exactly what the price covers.

Autopro must support variable-price services with quote requests.

Variable service pricing can depend on:

- Vehicle type
- Vehicle brand
- Vehicle model
- Engine/powertrain
- Service provider price list
- Add-ons
- Mobile service distance
- Emergency level
- Parts needed
- Labor needed

If a client already added a vehicle to their garage, Autopro should calculate service prices using that vehicle data where possible.

If the client has not added the vehicle, the client must manually select vehicle details before seeing accurate pricing where needed.

Service add-ons should be itemized.

Example:

Car wash: 20
Wax: 15
Interior cleaning: 12
Total: 47

The client can accept or decline the final service selection.

The provider can approve or adjust the booking depending on schedule and rules.

Mobile service area must be shown.

If a client is far away, extra travel fees can be added according to provider settings.

The client can accept or decline the extra fee.

# 24. Emergency And Towing Flow

Towing/recovery must be treated as a service provider category.

Autopro must support emergency service flow for vehicle breakdowns.

A client can use an emergency option and select the problem they are facing.

The client vehicle details should connect automatically if the vehicle is saved in the garage.

The system can notify nearby eligible garages, mobile mechanics, or towing/recovery providers depending on the issue.

If the car is broken on the road, the emergency flow should help connect the client with nearby towing providers.

The client should select the destination for the vehicle before pricing.

The system can estimate distance and show approximate pricing based on towing providers in the area.

The client location should stay protected until the client confirms a provider.

The client should see available towing options with:

- Provider name
- Verification
- Current distance/location estimate where allowed
- Estimated arrival time
- Price or price range
- Service terms
- Reviews/trust signals
- Contact/confirmation flow

A client can send requests to multiple providers in an emergency.

The client chooses which provider to confirm.

The towing provider must accept the price and destination before the flow opens full communication.

After provider acceptance, the client confirms.

Then direct communication can open.

The towing provider can set an arrival estimate.

Example:

I will arrive in 20 minutes.

A trusted garage can request or arrange towing for a VIP/trusted/elderly client if the client allows it.

A client can also schedule non-emergency towing or vehicle pickup for a later date.

If the client already has their own towing service, they can schedule directly with the garage.

Autopro should support proven towing platform features such as:

- Live driver location where legal
- Estimated arrival time
- Destination-based pricing
- Vehicle type selection
- Photo upload of vehicle situation
- Safety notes
- Roadside assistance category
- Emergency contact flow
- Driver verification
- Tow completion confirmation
- QR/manual confirmation
- Damage proof before and after tow
- Service receipt
- Review after completion
